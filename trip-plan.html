Japan

Sept 24 – 30 · 6 days · Tokyo + Kyoto + Osaka
Day 1 Arrive Tokyo Wednesday, Sept 24
Arrival
  • Land at Narita (NRT) or Haneda (HND)
  • Activate JR Pass at airport JR office (if purchased)
  • Pick up Suica or Pasmo IC card at airport — load ¥3,000 to start
  • Take Narita Express (N'EX) or Haneda Monorail into the city
  • Check in — recommended area: Shinjuku
Evening
  • Dinner at a nearby izakaya — yakitori skewers, cold Sapporo
  • Light stroll through Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane) if energy allows
Keep Day 1 easy. Jet lag going west→east hits hard. You'll be up at 4 am regardless — save energy for tomorrow.
Day 2 Tokyo — Harajuku · Shibuya · Shinjuku Thursday, Sept 25
Morning
  • Meiji Jingu Shrine — serene forested shrine in the heart of the city; go early
  • Harajuku / Takeshita Street — youth fashion, crepes, wild storefronts
Afternoon
  • Shibuya Scramble Crossing — the world's busiest pedestrian crossing
  • Shibuya Sky observation deck — 360° views; book timed entry online in advance
Evening
  • Dinner in Shinjuku — counter ramen or conveyor belt sushi
  • Golden Gai — maze of tiny atmospheric bars; each seats ~8 people; just walk in
Day 3 Tokyo — Asakusa · Akihabara · TeamLab Friday, Sept 26
Morning
  • Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa — Tokyo's oldest temple; arrive by 8 am before crowds
  • Browse Nakamise-dori shopping street — ningyo-yaki cakes, folding fans, daruma dolls
  • Walk along the Sumida River waterfront
Afternoon
  • Akihabara — electronics, retro games, anime goods; great for browsing even without the hobby
Evening
  • TeamLab Planets (Toyosu) — immersive digital art, ~1.5 hrs; must be pre-booked, sells out weeks ahead
  • Dinner in Toyosu or back in central Tokyo
Day 4 Day Trip — Kamakura Saturday, Sept 27
All Day
  • Take JR Yokosuka Line from Tokyo to Kamakura (~1 hr)
  • Kotoku-in — the 13-meter Great Buddha (Daibutsu); you can step inside the statue
  • Hase-dera Temple — tiered gardens and coastal views over the Pacific
  • Walk the Daibutsu Hiking Trail through forested hills to Kita-Kamakura
  • Hokoku-ji Temple — bamboo grove garden; matcha served on site
  • Late lunch / early dinner in Kamakura town before returning to Tokyo
Alternative: Nikko (ornate Toshogu Shrine, waterfalls, cedar avenues) — also ~2 hrs from Tokyo, more grand and mountainous vs. Kamakura's seaside calm.
Day 5 Shinkansen to Kyoto + Fushimi Inari Sunday, Sept 28
Morning
  • Late breakfast in Tokyo; check out of hotel
  • Board Tokaido Shinkansen from Tokyo Station → Kyoto Station
  • ~2h 15min on Nozomi / ~2h 40min on Hikari (JR Pass covers Hikari, not Nozomi)
  • Arrive Kyoto midday; check in — recommended: Gion or Higashiyama area
Afternoon / Evening
  • Fushimi Inari Taisha — thousands of torii gates winding up a mountain; allow 2–4 hrs
  • Go late afternoon for softer light and fewer crowds at the upper gates
  • Return to Gion district; dinner: kaiseki (multi-course Japanese cuisine) — worth splurging once
Day 6 Kyoto — Arashiyama · Kinkaku-ji · Pontocho Monday, Sept 29
Morning
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove — arrive before 8 am to beat the tour groups
  • Tenryu-ji — World Heritage Zen garden with koi ponds and raked gravel
  • Optional: rent a bicycle and explore Okochi Sanso garden, Jojakko-ji temple
Afternoon
  • Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) — iconic gold-leaf temple; mid-afternoon is slightly less crowded
  • Ryoan-ji — Japan's most famous rock garden; genuinely meditative
  • Nishiki Market ("Kyoto's Kitchen") — narrow covered market; try tsukemono pickles, tamagoyaki, matcha mochi
Evening
  • Dinner in Pontocho Alley — narrow lantern-lit lane of restaurants along the Kamo River
  • Evening walk through Gion — may spot maiko (apprentice geisha) after 8 pm
Day 7 Osaka Morning → Fly to Seoul Tuesday, Sept 30
Morning
  • Train from Kyoto to Osaka (~15 min, JR Kyoto Line or Hankyu)
  • Store luggage in coin lockers at Namba or Shin-Osaka
  • Dotonbori canal — neon signage, takoyaki (octopus balls), okonomiyaki; the iconic Glico Running Man sign
  • Optional: Osaka Castle park for a quieter morning alternative
Afternoon
  • Head to Kansai International Airport (KIX) — allow 60–90 min from Namba by Nankai Rapid
  • Afternoon/early evening flight: KIX → ICN Incheon (~2 hrs)
  • Arrive Seoul; take AREX Airport Railroad to city center (~43 min direct to Seoul Station)
  • Check in — recommended: Myeongdong or Hongdae
Book the KIX→ICN flight as early as possible — there are many airlines (Korean Air, Asiana, Peach, Jeju Air) but afternoon slots fill up. Target 3–5 pm departure.

South Korea

Sept 30 – Oct 5 · 5 days · Seoul
Day 8 Seoul — Gyeongbokgung · Bukchon · Myeongdong Wednesday, Oct 1
Morning
  • Gyeongbokgung Palace — the grandest Joseon dynasty palace (1395); arrives 9 am sharp
  • Rent a hanbok at the gates — free palace entry in traditional dress
  • Watch the Royal Guard Changing Ceremony at 10 am or 2 pm daily
  • National Folk Museum of Korea inside palace grounds — free with palace entry
Afternoon
  • Bukchon Hanok Village — hillside neighborhood of 600-year-old traditional Korean houses; excellent photos
  • Insadong — antiques, Korean crafts, tea houses, Ssamziegil courtyard market
Evening
  • Myeongdong street food — tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), hotteok (sweet pancakes), Korean fried chicken
  • Beauty shopping: Olive Young, Innisfree, Etude House are all clustered here
Day 9 DMZ Tour + Gangnam Evening Thursday, Oct 2
Morning / Afternoon (Full Day)
  • DMZ (Demilitarized Zone) Tour — must book in advance via licensed operator (USO, Koridoor, or Viator operators)
  • Half-day: 3rd Infiltration Tunnel, Dora Observatory, Dorasan Station
  • Full-day also includes Imjingak Peace Park and optional JSA (Joint Security Area) — JSA requires separate advance booking with additional passport requirements
  • Tours depart Seoul around 8 am, return by 1–5 pm depending on type
Evening (Back in Seoul ~5–6 pm)
  • Gangnam — upscale district; dinner at a Korean BBQ restaurant
  • COEX Starfield Library — stunning two-story underground bookshelf installation inside COEX Mall; free to enter
  • Bongeunsa Temple next to COEX — ancient temple with skyscrapers as backdrop; open until 9 pm
Day 10 Hongdae + Han River Friday, Oct 3
Morning
  • Hongdae (Hongik University area) — street murals, independent boutiques, excellent cafes, morning bakeries
  • Grab coffee at a specialty roaster; the café culture here is exceptional
Afternoon
  • Yeouido Han River Park — rent a bike along the river or join locals with a picnic; grab banana milk from a convenience store
  • Optional: Lotte World — indoor/outdoor theme park; fun if you want something energetic
Evening
  • Noryangjin Fish Market — massive 24-hr market; choose your live seafood and take it upstairs to a restaurant that cooks it for you
  • Return to Hongdae for the nightlife scene if energy allows
Day 11 N Seoul Tower · DDP · Gwangjang Market Saturday, Oct 4
Morning
  • Namsan Mountain — take the cable car up or hike the trail (30 min forested walk)
  • N Seoul Tower — observation deck with panoramic views over all of Seoul; the "locks of love" fence
Afternoon
  • Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) — futuristic Zaha Hadid building; rotating design exhibitions
  • Dongdaemun Market surrounding DDP — vast clothing and textile wholesale market; great for fashion browsing
Evening
  • Gwangjang Market — Seoul's oldest traditional market (est. 1905); famous for bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes), mayak kimbap (addictive mini rice rolls), and raw beef yukhoe
  • Final evening drinks in Hongdae or Itaewon
Day 12 Depart Seoul (ICN) Sunday, Oct 5
Morning
  • Final morning shopping at Myeongdong or Insadong
  • Good picks: Olive Young skincare haul, Korean instant noodles, honey butter chips, Korean teas, gochujang paste
Airport
  • Head to Incheon International Airport (ICN) — allow at least 3 hours before international departure
  • AREX from Seoul Station: 43 min direct express / 66 min all-stop — direct is worth the extra ₩4,500
  • Incheon has excellent duty-free shopping — budget 30–45 min post-security

Money

Packing Notes

Shoes15,000–25,000 steps/day. Bring broken-in walking shoes. Non-negotiable.
LayersLate Sept/early Oct: 10–25°C range. Pack light layers — a shell jacket, mid-layer, and t-shirts.
Power AdaptersJapan: Type A (same as US). Korea: Type C/F (EU plug). Bring a universal adapter or two.
Slip-on ShoesMany shrines and some restaurants require removing shoes. Slip-ons save significant time.
Day BagA lightweight packable tote or small daypack for market hauls and day trips.
Cash WalletKeep a separate small wallet for cash — Japan is heavily cash-based.
